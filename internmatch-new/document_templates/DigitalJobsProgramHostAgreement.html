Conditions of Subsidy

  1. The Host Employer has applied to participate in the Digital Jobs Program (Program) and host one or more Interns for subsidised Internships of 12 weeks or as otherwise provided in this Agreement.

  2. The Department has engaged Hudson to manage the Program on its behalf. Hudson has written to the Host Employer by email (Internship Confirmation Email) confirming that:

    1. the Host Employer has been matched with one or more Interns

    2. offering, on behalf of the Department, that the Host Employer may participate in the Program by employing the Intern/s or engaging the Intern/s through a Labour Hire Agency and receiving the Subsidy, and

    3. providing the Intern Host Handbook.

  3. The Internship Confirmation Email from Hudson, on behalf of the Department, to the Host Employer constitutes an offer by the Department to the Host Employer. In the event the Host Employer accepts this offer by acknowledging and agreeing to these Conditions of Subsidy, an agreement is formed between the Host Employer and the Department (Agreement) which includes:

    1. the Internship Confirmation Email from Hudson

    2. these Conditions of Subsidy

    3. the Intern Host Handbook

    4. the information supplied by the Host Employer in the Application.

  4. In these Conditions of Subsidy, the following definitions apply:

    • Application means the application submitted by the Host Employer to the Department to participate in the Program, by undertaking one or more Internships and to receive a Subsidy.

    • Department or State means the State of Victoria through its Department of Jobs, Precincts and Regions

    • Host Employer means the applicant specified above, which has applied to the Department to participate in the Program, by undertaking one or more Internships and to receive a Subsidy.

    • Hudson means Hudson Global Resources (Aust) Pty Ltd, ABN 21 002 888 762 which has been engaged by the Department to manage the Program on its behalf.

    • Intern means the participants in the Program who are selected by Hudson to receive training, work experience and mentoring to increase their skills and who are offered to the Host Employer for an Internship.

    • Internship means the employment or engagement through a Labour Hire Agency of one or more Interns by the Host Employer for a minimum of 12 weeks or as otherwise provided in this Agreement full-time (or part-time equivalent where agreed to by the Intern) for which the Subsidy is provided on the terms of this Agreement.

    • Labour Hire Agency means a labour hire provider as defined in section 7 of the Labour Hire Licensing Act 2018 (Vic.), being a business that has an arrangement in place with the Intern to supply the Intern to perform work in and as part of the Host Employer’s business or undertaking and is obliged to pay the Intern for the work performed for the Host Employer.

    • Program means the Digital Jobs Program.

    • Subsidy means the amount provided by Hudson to the Host Employer under this Agreement for the Internship.

    • Survey means the survey required to be completed by the Host Employer to provide information to the Department on the conduct and outcomes of the Internship, as set out in clause 10.

    • Training Course means the training course arranged by Hudson and undertaken by the Intern as part of the Program.

  5. The Agreement commences on the date the Host Employer acknowledges and agrees to these Conditions of Subsidy and expires when the Internship is completed, and the Host Employer has provided all information requested by the Department and Hudson pursuant to clause 10. Clauses 16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22, 23 and 24 survive expiry or termination of the Agreement.

  6. Hudson will pay the Host Employer a Subsidy of $5,000 (or pro rata amount if Internship term reduced), subject to the terms and conditions of the Agreement, in the manner set out in the payment schedule in clause 11.

  7. The Host Employer must use the Subsidy only to subsidise the Intern’s wages in the course of their employment with the Host Employer or payments to the Labour Hire Agency for the engagement of the Intern.

  8. The Host Employer will employ the Intern, or engage the Intern through a Labour Hire Agency, in a paid role that:

    1. provides the Intern an opportunity to learn and apply the digital skills they have obtained from their Training Course;

    2. is relevant to the subject area they have studied in their Training Course; and

    3. is ideally project or product-based with defined deliverables.

  9. The Host Employer will:

    1. enter into a formal employment contract with the Intern or engage a Labour Hire Agency to provide the Intern on the Minimum Terms of Employment (Employment Contract).

    2. employ the Intern, or ensure that the Labour Hire Agency employs the Intern, in accordance with the following minimum terms (to be recorded in the Employment Contract) (Minimum Terms of Employment):

      1. duration of employment term must be a minimum of 12 weeks full-time (or part-time equivalent where agreed to by the Intern), unless the Intern’s employment term runs to the end of the calendar year, in which case the Intern’s employment may be concluded on 24 December by agreement with the intern

      2. terms must comply with all industrial relations obligations for employers under the National Employment Standards in accordance with the Fair Work Act (2009) Cth; and

      3. the Intern must be paid at least the relevant minimum wage.

    3. comply with, and ensure that the Labour Hire Agency (if relevant) complies with, the Occupational Health and Safety Act 2004 (Vic), Equal Opportunity Act 2010 (Vic), Workplace Injury Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2013 (Vic), all applicable superannuation guarantee legislation and all WorkSafe compliance codes and codes of practice in the employment of the Intern (visit https://www.worksafe.vic.gov.au/compliance-codes-and-codes-practice).

    4. report any work health and safety incidents regarding the Intern to Hudson within two working days.

    5. be responsible for day-to-day management of the Intern and for all and any obligations regarding or in connection with the Intern, including requiring the Labour Hire Agency to comply with its obligations as an employer (if relevant).

    6. provide the Intern adequate support, including:

      1. a suitable manager/supervisor to provide direction and support to the Intern whilst work is being undertaken, and the provision of contact details; and

      2. an appropriate induction and onboarding phase, providing relevant briefings on safety standards, site access, normal hours of work, emergency details and other elements as appropriate.

    7. supply the Intern with necessary equipment and resources for undertaking the Internship.

    8. in delivering the employment opportunity for the Intern, comply with, and ensure that the Labour Hire Agency (if relevant) complies with, the employer expectations and requirements set out in the Intern Host Handbook.

  10. The Host Employer must provide to the Department such information as the Department or Hudson may request from time to time in relation to the Internship, the Host Employer’s activities and the expenditure of the Subsidy, including information in relation to the evaluation of the Internship. In particular, the Host Employer must provide the following to Hudson:

    1. within one week of the completion of the Intern’s employment/engagement period, a Survey confirming information about the Intern’s employment including:

      1. the Intern has been provided with an opportunity to learn and apply the digital skills they have obtained from their training course;

      2. the Intern’s employment has been relevant to the subject area they have studied in their Training Course; and

      3. the Intern’s employment has been project or product-based with defined deliverables.

    2. Within one week of the completion of the Intern’s employment period, either a copy of the Intern’s payslips, invoices from the Labour Hire Agency or a Statutory Declaration (in the form of the template to be provided by Hudson), confirming the Intern has been employed by the Host Employer, or engaged by the Host Employer through a Labour Hire Agency, for a period of not less than 12 weeks or as otherwise provided in this Agreement on a full-time basis (or part time equivalent where agreed to by the Intern).

  11. Hudson will pay the Subsidy to the Host Employer in accordance with the following payment schedule:

  12. The Host Employer acknowledges and agrees that it is solely responsible and liable for the employment of the Intern, or engagement of the Intern through a Labour Hire Agency, and that the Host Employer and the Labour Hire Agency are responsible for all payments due to the Intern, howsoever arising, including without limitation for base rates of pay (and related on-costs such as annual leave and personal leave) and incentive-based payments and bonuses, loadings, monetary allowances, overtime or penalty rates, and any other separately identifiable amounts as well as for paying superannuation, payroll or any other tax, WorkCover levy or any similar payments or entitlements in relation to the Intern.

  13. The Host Employer must promptly advise Hudson if the Host Employer will not or may not perform or complete the Internship. In such event the Department may require the refund of the whole or part of the Subsidy as it determines appropriate, in its absolute discretion.

  14. Nothing contained in the Agreement is to be construed as creating any obligation, commitment or undertaking by the Department or Hudson to provide additional or further funding or assistance beyond that provided in this Agreement.

The Host Employer agrees that nothing in this agreement creates an employment relationship between Hudson or the Victorian government and the Intern.

  17. The Host Employer warrants that any intellectual property used by it in connection with the Survey and any other documents and materials provided by the Host Employer to the Department under this Agreement are the sole property of the Host Employer or the Host Employer is legally entitled to use that intellectual property for that purpose.

  18. The Host Employer authorises and consents to the Department holding, using and disclosing personal information in the Survey and any other documents and materials provided by the Host Employer to the Department and Hudson under this Agreement to other State government departments and agencies and where required, to the Department’s external consultants, for the purposes of administering and reviewing the Internship and the Program, evaluating the Program and for policy development and planning purposes in accordance with the Department’s processes and the Privacy and Data Protection Act 2014 (Vic).

  19. Upon request, the Host Employer must permit the Department and/or the Auditor General of Victoria access to accounting and employment records relating to the Internship.

  20. If the Host Employer breaches the Agreement or if the Department is of the reasonable opinion that:

    1. the Internship is not proceeding satisfactorily; or

    2. the Host Employer has engaged or may engage in any conduct which affects or may adversely affect the goodwill or reputation of the Host Employer, the Internship, the Department, a Minister, or the State,

    3. the Subsidy have not been expended solely on the Internship; or

    4. the Host Employer has provided any false or misleading information in the application process,

    the Department may in its absolute discretion-:

    1. withhold any payment due to be made under the Subsidy

    2. require the repayment to Hudson of the whole or part of the Subsidy as may be determined by the Department in its absolute discretion; and/or

    3. by written notice terminate this Agreement.

  21. Where the Department is of the reasonable opinion that-:

    1. there has been a significant deterioration in the financial circumstances of the Host Employer;

    2. the Host Employer is subject to an insolvency administration; or

    3. continued association with the Internship or the Host Employer may bring a Minister, the Department or the State into disrepute,

    the Department may in its absolute discretion, require Hudson to withhold, suspend, cancel or terminate any payment or payments due or to fall due and/or by written notice terminate the Agreement.

  22. The Department reserves the right to publicise the benefits accruing as the result of Internship. The Host Employer must cooperate with the Department in relation to all publicity associated with the Internship.

  23. The Department may include the name of the Host Employer in its annual report.

  24. The Host Employer hereby indemnifies the Department, its officers, employees and agents against any cost, loss, liability or expense incurred by the Department arising from any act or omission of the Host Employer in connection with the Internship or breach of this Agreement by the Host Employer.

  25. Any variations to these conditions or to the Internship must be in writing and agreed by both parties.

  26. Each provision of these conditions (or part thereof) will, unless the context requires otherwise, be read and construed as a separate or severable provision so that if any provision or part thereof is void or otherwise unenforceable for any reason then that provision or part thereof, will be severed and the remainder will be read and construed as if the severable provision or part thereof, had never existed.

  27. The laws of the State of Victoria shall govern these conditions and the parties submit themselves to the jurisdiction of the courts in the State of Victoria.
