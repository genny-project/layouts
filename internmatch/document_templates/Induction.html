STUDENT INFORMATION
INTRODUCTION
Welcome to the Professional Year Internship Program. Your work-based learning will focus on linking your knowledge and skills with situations in the workplace. It will provide you with the opportunity to put theory into practice and make you more ready for work.
WHAT IS AN INTERNSHIP?
An internship is a structured workplace learning experience that prepares you for the workforce. The experience with the placement provider will:
  • Enable you to gain real-life experience in the workplace and improve communication skills;
  • Help you link knowledge and skills with jobs; and
  • Assist you into better understand theory learnt by putting it into practice.
WHAT ARE THE ADVANTAGES?
The advantages of the Internship component are:
  • Learn, develop and apply knowledge and skills relevant to the workplace;
  • Achieve skills that are recognised by industry;
  • Obtain skills that will assist you in seeking employment;
  • Gain knowledge of employers’ expectations; and
  • Make contacts with employers.
  • Build your Network
WHAT ARE THE INTERNS RESPONSIBILITIES?
ATTITUDES & BEHAVIOURS
You will need to:
  • Show enthusiasm and initiative;
  • Accept and complete duties assigned;
  • Be willing to learn;
  • Listen to instructions and ask questions;
  • Accept and act on advice given;
  • Ask for jobs when you have nothing to do;
  • Be polite, courteous and well-mannered;
  • Display safe practice by abiding by the policies and procedures of the placement provider;
  • Avoid distracting other employees unnecessarily; and
  • Dress appropriately to industry standards.
ATTENDANCE AND PUNCTUALITY
The Intern will be expected to:
  • Attend your internship placement during work hours and be prepared to start your placement on time each day; take only the allocated time for meal breaks and return promptly to your work; and
  • Contact your workplace supervisor and your Internship Placement Coordinator immediately if you are unable to attend.
WHAT IF I AM ILL OR CANNOT ATTEND?
If you are unable to attend your scheduled internship placement for any reason, you must inform your workplace supervisor as well as the ATMC Internship Placement Coordinator. You must submit documentary evidence such as a medical certificate to substantiate your non-attendance.
CONFIDENTIALITY
You may be exposed to information about the business and clients that will be confidential. You will be expected to maintain privacy by not repeating any of this information. In some cases, employers may want you to sign a confidentiality contract. Discuss with your workplace supervisor what is considered confidential.
WHAT YOU NEED TO DO TO SUCCEED?
Take responsibility for your actions, discuss learning needs and concerns with your workplace supervisor, continually reflect on your performance and ensure you obtain adequate feedback on your performance from your workplace supervisor.
PLACEMENT ORIENTATION
By the end of your first day, the intern and supervisor need to read the checklist below and sign at the bottom.
ORIENTATION CHECKLIST
  1. Completed induction/tour of the workplace.
  2. Verified dates for placement and start/finish/break times.
  3. Identified workplace supervisor (and other key staff).
  4. Discussed any specific issues or concerns.
  5. Aware of evacuation and fire safety arrangements.
    1. Emergency phone numbers;
    2. Extinguishers/fire blankets;
    3. Exit signs and assembly points; and
    4. Understand my responsibilities in the event of an emergency.
  6. Aware of Incident / Accident arrangements.
    1. Know who to report to and which documentation to complete.
  7. Aware of organisation’s privacy and confidentiality requirements.
  8. Aware of organisation’s complaints policy and procedure.
