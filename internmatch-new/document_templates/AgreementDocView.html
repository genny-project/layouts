Work Integrated Learning (WIL) Internship Agreement

  1. General Terms and Conditions

    1. The purpose of this agreement is to ensure a productive and successful internship experience for all parties.
    2. The internship is an opportunity for the Work Integrated Learning ("WIL") students to gain practical work experience with the Host Company (place where the internship is conducted).
    3. The internship does not constitute an Employment agreement or any offer of employment with the Host Company.
    4. Any financial support or transport allowances may be offered at the Host Company's discretion. However, a Host Company cannot charge any fees or expenses to the Intern for this work experience to take place.
    5. The Internship will commence only after an agreed commencement date and end date is determined by the Host Company with the Intern and approved by Outcome.Life (this form).
    6. The Host Company will provide a training plan clearly outlining the range of duties to be performed and the range of skills and competencies required for the WIL Student during the course of the internship.
    7. The Host Company will assign an appropriate Supervisor to mentor the WIL Student.
    8. The Supervisor will be present and available to support the intern with any queries for the majority of their internship placement. Should the assigned intern supervisor not be available, an appropriate qualified replacement will be assigned.
    9. The internship will take place in a suitably located, accessible and suitable workplace. Home office locations do not meet the requirements of an approved internship.
    10. Upon Completion of the Internship Program, the Host Company may provide a letter of reference outlining the tasks and competencies of the intern although is not required to do so.
    11. Should the Host company consider a WIL Student is in breach of attendance, performance and/or any other company policy, they may report their concerns by contacting Outcome.Life. The company can request that the internship be terminated or alternatively the company may choose to counsel the intern to rectify the issue.
    12. The intern agrees to dress appropriately to work and meet the other standards that the Host Company sets for its employees.
    13. All intellectual property rights in all work performed by interns for the Host Company become on creation, the property of the Host Company.
    14. Outcome.Life grants approval that if a Host Company has an Internal Intellectual property policy, they can request this form be signed by the intern.
    15. The Host Company will have in place documentation outlining their OH&S policies and procedures.
    16. The Host Company confirms that it complies with applicable laws in the conduct of its business, including those related to OH&S and use and operation of its premises.
    17. All WIL Students provided by Outcome.Life are comprehensively covered by the Outcome.Life Voluntary Workers insurance for the duration of the agreed internship period.
    18. Any days of absence throughout the internship needs to be made up within the provided dates. Medical certificates must be provided for any days missed due to illness or injury.
