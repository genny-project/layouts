The below Terms & Conditions are valid for 12 months from the date of signing and as such you should retain this document for your reference.

From commencement, the Internship programs are unpaid and considered and defined to be vocational placements under the Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth). Although Interns may participate in the performance of productive activities during Internship Placements, there remains no entitlement to, and will not receive, any remuneration in respect of the Internship Placement or work undertaken during the Internship Placement.

Internship Placements do not represent an intention to, and do not, create an employment relationship of any kind and accordingly, Interns will not (for any purpose) become an employee of the Host Company during the period of an Internship Placement.

1. Internship Placement Terms and Conditions

1.1 Nature of the Internship Placement

The Internship Placement is unpaid. The Internship Placement is a Vocational Placement and as such, the Intern has no entitlement to any remuneration in respect of the Internship Placement or work undertaken during the Internship Placement. The Host Company is under no obligation to provide the Intern with any remuneration during the term of, or in respect of any work performed by the Intern during, the Internship Placement.

Interns are approved to only complete an Internship Placement with the above registered company or commonly controlled entities. Interns are not allowed to undertake an Internship Placement, employment or an unremunerated Internship Placement with a different Host Company.

1.2 Internship Placement extension

Under no circumstances is the Internship Placement to be extended beyond the specified Start and End Dates. Internship dates will be emailed to you upon confirmation of a specific Internship Placement.

1.3 Commitment to training

Host Companies and Interns must maintain the agreed Training Plan developed prior to the commencement of the Internship Placement. The Training Plan identifies the types of duties the Intern will be introduced to and as such, is a practical base that will ensure the Intern obtains relevant learning opportunities, while also ensuring that they develop skills and knowledge that are relevant to your business.

1.4 Supervision

Host Companies agree to provide adequate supervision to the Intern throughout the Internship Placement. This involves:

  1. consistently monitoring your Intern's learning progress
  2. appointing an adequately qualified staff member to the supervisor role
  3. providing training as required
  4. ensuring appropriate procedures and processes are in place that support a positive learning environment for the Intern
  5. communicating any changes or circumstances of the Internship Placement to Outcome.Life immediately
  6. ensuring that the Host Company has a demonstrated commitment to a workplace that is free from bullying and discrimination, and immediately reporting to Outcome.Life any complaint or incident involving the Intern in respect of bullying or discrimination in the workplace
  7. providing an induction program, to ensure that the Intern is aware of and familiar with the obligations of safe work, policies and procedures that apply to the Host Company's business and operations, including the procedure for notifying health and safety issues at the Host Company; and
  8. ensuring, as far as is reasonably practicable, that the Intern is not exposed to risks to their health and safety arising from the conduct of the undertaking of the Host Company

1.5 Other obligations

Outcome.Life appreciates the time involved in training and supporting the Interns. The Host Company is obliged to notify Outcome.Life immediately if any of the following circumstances change:

  1. hours of work
  2. attendance issues (i.e. sick days, punctuality issues, or other)
  3. changes to the Training Plan (i.e. changes to the scope or objectives of the Internship Placement)
  4. human resources issues that may affect the Internship Placement or supervision of the Intern. For example, the departure of the Intern's immediate supervisor.
  5. any other changes

1.6 Obligations of Outcome.Life

Outcome.Life acknowledges that it must:

  1. a) provide an induction program, for the Intern who will undertake the Internship Placement, informing them of their general responsibility to behave in a safe manner in a workplace environment and comply with the requirements of occupational health and safety legislation; and
  2. b) remain responsible for the overall control and discipline of the Intern, apart from reasonable direction provided on a day to day basis by the Host Company to the Intern while they are undertaking the Internship Placement at the Host Company's premises.

2. Guidelines for Interns and Host Companies

Adhering to the following guidelines will ensure a successful and a most rewarding experience for the Intern and the Host Company.

2.1 Punctuality and attendance

Outcome.Life will check with the Host Company and the Intern on the progress and attendance of the Intern on a regular basis. Any required leave must be agreed to by Outcome.Life in advance. Any absence due to sickness must be reported to the Placement Consultant and Supervisor at the Host Company immediately.

2.2 Dress code

The Intern must conform to the dress code of the Host Company.

2.3 Confidentiality

Interns agree to keep all information and documents entrusted to them confidential and in accordance with Host Company procedures and ethics.

2.4 Intellectual Property

All intellectual property in all materials created by the Intern or Host Company during the Internship Placement will be and remain the property of the Host Company.

2.5 Misuse of Host Company facilities and property

Any misuse of Host Company facilities or property is prohibited and the Intern shall be liable for any expenses incurred as a result of such misuse. It is the responsibility of the Intern to ensure that they understand Host Company policy regarding the use of mobile phones, Internet access and phone usage for personal use during Internship Placement hours. The Intern must conform to these policies.

2.6 Conduct

The Intern agrees to carry out all assigned tasks and to approach every opportunity provided by the Host Company with enthusiasm and professionalism to the best of their ability.

2.7 Issues

In the first instance, issues that arise should be discussed with the supervisor at the Host Company. If the issue is not resolved, Outcome.Life will make every effort possible to facilitate and discuss the matter with the Intern and Host Company.

2.8 Liability and insurance

All Interns are fully covered for public liability, professional indemnity and student personal accident insurance by either the Education Provider the student is enrolled with or Outcome.Life. Medical / travel insurance is the sole responsibility of the Intern and in no way, is a legal responsibility of the Host Company or Outcome.Life. Whilst Outcome.Life may assist in the arrangement of such insurance, it is the Intern's responsibility to ensure that valid and appropriate cover is in place.

2.9 Early cessation of the Internship Placement

The Host Company reserves the right to cease hosting the Intern prior to the End Date of the Internship Placement in circumstances where it reasonably believes there to be a legitimate reason for such cessation. The Host Company agrees to advise Outcome.Life, prior to any decision to cease hosting the Intern, of the reasons why it wishes to cease the Internship Placement and to only cease hosting the Intern if the issue cannot be resolved. As this is a training opportunity, any issues whether poor performance or attitude are required to be identified and discussed with Outcome.Life so that the Intern is given an opportunity to improve.

3. Acknowledgement of Internship Relationship and Role of Both Parties.

Signing of this confirmation document is an acknowledgement by your organisation (the Host Company) that:

  1. Internship placements (workplace-based training or special program placements) are designed to assist Interns to provide a cultural/social experience and/or to develop skills relevant to their tertiary education, current occupation or field of study. It will involve the observation of tasks performed by employees of the Host Company and/or the performance of tasks under close supervision of the employees of the Host Company.
  2. There is no intention on the part of the Host Company or the Intern to create an employment relationship as recognised under the law of Australia. It is accepted by the Intern that there is no employment relationship between the Host Company and the Intern.
  3. The Host Company understands its obligations under Australian industrial relations laws if the Intern becomes an employee
  4. The Host Company understands that workplace relations laws make it unlawful to employ an employee and not pay them, and that penalties may be imposed against any employer who breaches these laws.
  5. The Host Company considers that this arrangement constitutes a vocational placement under the Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)
