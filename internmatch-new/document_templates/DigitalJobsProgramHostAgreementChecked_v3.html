The Department agrees to provide the Subsidy to the Host Employer on the following terms:

  1. The Host Employer has applied to participate in the Program and host one or more Participants for subsidised Work Placements of 12 weeks or as otherwise provided in this Agreement.

  2. The Department has engaged the Delivery Partner to manage select services within the Program on its behalf. The Delivery Partner has written to the Host Employer by email (Placement Confirmation Email) confirming that:

    1. the Host Employer has been matched with one or more Participants;

    2. offering, on behalf of the Department, that the Host Employer may participate in the Program by employing the Participant/s or engaging the Participant/s through a Labour Hire Agency and receiving the Subsidy; and

    3. providing the Placement Host Handbook.

  3. The Host Employer may apply again in the future to participate in the Program and host one or more additional Participants for subsidised Work Placements of 12 weeks or as otherwise provided in this Agreement. In this event, the Delivery Partner may write to the Host Employer providing a Placement Confirmation Email with the details of the additional Participants with which the Host Employer has been matched (Additional Placement Confirmation Email).

  4. The Placement Confirmation Email from the Delivery Partner, on behalf of the Department, to the Host Employer constitutes an offer by the Department to the Host Employer. By agreeing to these terms, an agreement is formed between the Host Employer and the Department (Agreement)which includes:

    1. the Placement Confirmation Email from the Delivery Partner;

    2. any Additional Placement Confirmation Emails;

    3. these terms;

    4. the Placement Host Handbook; and

    5. the information supplied by the Host Employer in the current and any future Applications.

    In the event of inconsistency, the order of precedence set out above will apply.

  5. In this Agreement, the following definitions apply:

    1. Application means the application submitted by the Host Employer to the Department to participate in the Program, by undertaking one or more Work Placements and to receive a Subsidy, including both applications submitted before the date of this Agreement and any applications submitted in the future.

    2. Delivery Partner means Outcome.Life Pty Ltd ABN 89 608 648 826 trading as InternMatch Australia, which has been engaged by the Department to manage select services within the Program on its behalf.

    3. Department or State means the State of Victoria through its Department of Jobs, Precincts and Regions ABN 83 295 188 244.

    4. GST has the meaning given in the GST Law.

    5. GST Law means A New Tax System (Goods and Services Tax) Act 1999 (Cth).

    6. Host Employer (also referred to in the Program as an internship host and industry partner) means the applicant specified above, which has applied to the Department to participate in the Program, by undertaking one or more Work Placements and to receive a Subsidy.

    7. Labour Hire Agency means a labour hire provider as defined in section 7 of the Labour Hire Licensing Act 2018 (VIC), being a business that has an arrangement in place with the Participant to supply the Participant to perform work in and as part of the Host Employer’s business or undertaking and is obliged to pay the Participant for the work performed for the Host Employer.

    8. Participant (also referred to in the Program as a candidate) means the participants in the Program who are selected by the Delivery Partner to receive training, work experience and mentoring to increase their skills and who are offered to the Host Employer for a Work Placement.

    9. Program means the Department’s Digital Jobs program.

    10. Subsidy means the amount provided by the Delivery Partner to the Host Employer under this Agreement as a subsidy for Participant wages in connection with a Work Placement.

    11. Survey means the survey required to be completed by the Host Employer to provide information to the Department on the conduct and outcomes of the Work Placement, as set out in clause 11.

    12. Tax Invoice has the same meaning as specified in the GST Law.

    13. Taxable Supply has the same meaning as specified in the GST Law.

    14. Training Course means the training course arranged by the Delivery Partner and undertaken by the Participant as part of the Program.

    15. Work Placement (also referred to in the Program as an internship and a placement) means the employment or engagement through a Labour Hire Agency of one or more Participants by the Host Employer for a minimum of 12 weeks or as otherwise provided in this Agreement full-time (or part-time equivalent where agreed to by the Participant) for which the Subsidy is provided on the terms of this Agreement.

  6. The Agreement commences on the date the Agreement is signed and expires when the Work Placement is completed, and the Host Employer has provided all information requested by the Department and the Delivery Partner pursuant to clause 11. Clauses 17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22, 23, 24 and 27 survive expiry or termination of the Agreement.

  7. The Delivery Partner will pay the Host Employer a Subsidy of $5,000 (ex GST) (or pro rata amount if Work Placement term reduced), subject to the terms and conditions of the Agreement, in the manner set out in the payment schedule in clause 12.

  8. The Host Employer must use the Subsidy only to subsidise the Participant’s wages in the course of their employment with the Host Employer or payments to the Labour Hire Agency for the engagement of the Participant.

  9. The Host Employer will employ the Participant, or engage the Participant through a Labour Hire Agency, in a paid role that:

    1. provides the Participant an opportunity to learn and apply the digital skills they have obtained from their Training Course;

    2. is relevant to the subject area they have studied in their Training Course; and

    3. is ideally project or product-based with defined deliverables.

  10. The Host Employer will:

    1. enter into a formal employment contract with the Participant or engage a Labour Hire Agency to provide the Participant on the Minimum Terms of Employment (Employment Contract);

    2. employ the Participant, or ensure that the Labour Hire Agency employs the Participant, in accordance with the following minimum terms (to be recorded in the Employment Contract) (Minimum Terms of Employment):

      1. duration of employment term must be a minimum of 12 weeks full-time (or part-time equivalent where agreed to by the Participant), unless the Participant’s employment term runs to the end of the calendar year, in which case the Participant’s employment may be concluded on 24 December by agreement with the intern;

      2. terms must comply with all industrial relations obligations for employers under the National Employment Standards in accordance with the Fair Work Act (2009) Cth;

      3. the Participant must be paid at least the relevant minimum wage;

    3. comply with, and ensure that the Labour Hire Agency (if relevant) complies with:

      1. the Occupational Health and Safety Act 2004 (VIC), Equal Opportunity Act 2010 (VIC) and Workplace Injury Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2013 (VIC) or equivalent legislation in the Host Employer’s jurisdiction;

      2. all applicable superannuation guarantee legislation; and

      3. all WorkSafe compliance codes and codes of practice in the employment of the Participant (visit https://www.worksafe.vic.gov.au/compliance-codes-and-codes-practice).

    4. report any work health and safety incidents regarding the Participant to the Delivery Partner within two working days;

    5. be responsible for day-to-day management of the Participant and for all and any obligations regarding or in connection with the Participant, including requiring the Labour Hire Agency to comply with its obligations as an employer (if relevant);

    6. provide the Participant adequate support, including:

      1. a suitable manager/supervisor to provide direction and support to the Participant whilst work is being undertaken, and the provision of contact details; and

      2. an appropriate induction and onboarding phase, providing relevant briefings on safety standards, site access, normal hours of work, emergency details and other elements as appropriate;

    7. supply the Participant with necessary equipment and resources for undertaking the Work Placement; and

    8. in delivering the employment opportunity for the Participant, comply with, and ensure that the Labour Hire Agency (if relevant) complies with, the employer expectations and requirements set out in the Placement Host Handbook.

  11. The Host Employer must provide to the Department such information as the Department or the Delivery Partner may request from time to time in relation to the Work Placement, the Host Employer’s activities and the expenditure of the Subsidy, including information in relation to the evaluation of the Work Placement. In particular, the Host Employer must provide the following to the Delivery Partner:

    1. within one week of the completion of the Participant’s employment/engagement period, a Survey confirming information about the Participant’s employment including:

      1. the Participant has been provided with an opportunity to learn and apply the digital skills they have obtained from their training course;

      2. the Participant’s employment has been relevant to the subject area they have studied in their Training Course; and

      3. the Participant’s employment has been project or product-based with defined deliverables; and

    2. within one week of the completion of the Participant’s employment period, either a copy of the Participant’s payslips, invoices from the Labour Hire Agency or a Statutory Declaration (in the form of the template to be provided by the Delivery Partner), confirming the Participant has been employed by the Host Employer, or engaged by the Host Employer through a Labour Hire Agency, for a period of not less than 12 weeks or as otherwise provided in this Agreement on a full-time basis (or part time equivalent where agreed to by the Participant).

  12. The Delivery Partner will pay the Subsidy in instalments to the Host Employer in accordance with the following payment schedule:

    Instalment When Payment requirements Amount
    1 Commencement of Work Placement

    • Signing of this Agreement by the Host Employer.

    • Provision of a fully signed and dated Employment Contract for the Work Placement.

    • Provision of a Tax Invoice addressed to the Delivery Partner for the amount of this instalment.

    		 $2,500.00 (ex GST)
    2 Completion of Work Placement

    • On completion of the Work Placement

    • Provision of a Tax Invoice addressed to the Delivery Partner for the amount of this instalment.

    • Provision by the Host Employer to the Delivery Partner (and Department if required) of:

      • Survey,

      • Pay slip, invoices from Labour Hire Agency or statutory declaration; and

      • any other information required by the Delivery Partner.

    		 $2,500.00 (ex GST) (or pro rata amount, as directed by the Department, if Work Placement term reduced)

    Total

    		 $5,000.00(ex GST) (or pro rata amount, as directed by the Department, if Work Placement term reduced)

  13. The Host Employer acknowledges and agrees that it is solely responsible and liable for the employment of the Participant, or engagement of the Participant through a Labour Hire Agency, and that the Host Employer and the Labour Hire Agency are responsible for all payments due to the Participant, howsoever arising, including without limitation for base rates of pay (and related on-costs such as annual leave and personal leave) and incentive-based payments and bonuses, loadings, monetary allowances, overtime or penalty rates, and any other separately identifiable amounts as well as for paying superannuation, payroll or any other tax, WorkCover levy or any similar payments or entitlements in relation to the Participant.

  14. The Host Employer must promptly advise the Delivery Partner if the Host Employer will not or may not perform or complete the Work Placement. In such event the Department may require the refund of the whole or part of the Subsidy as it determines appropriate, in its absolute discretion.

  15. Nothing contained in the Agreement is to be construed as creating any obligation, commitment or undertaking by the Department or the Delivery Partner to provide additional or further funding or assistance beyond that provided in this Agreement.

  16. Neither the Host Employer, nor its employees, servants or agents shall by reason of the Subsidy, be or become an agent of or in the service or employment of the State of Victoria in relation to the Work Placement or for any other purpose whatsoever. The Host Employer agrees that nothing in this agreement creates an employment relationship between the Delivery Partner or the Victorian government and the Participant.

  17. The Host Employer grants to the Department a non-exclusive, fully paid up licence to reproduce, publish or otherwise use for non-commercial purposes the Survey and any other documents and materials provided by the Host Employer under this Agreement.

  18. The Host Employer warrants that any intellectual property used by it in connection with the Survey and any other documents and materials provided by the Host Employer to the Department under this Agreement are the sole property of the Host Employer or the Host Employer is legally entitled to use that intellectual property for that purpose.

  19. The Host Employer authorises and consents to the Department holding, using and disclosing personal information in the Survey and any other documents and materials provided by the Host Employer to the Department and the Delivery Partner under this Agreement to other State government departments and agencies and where required, to the Department’s external consultants, for the purposes of administering and reviewing the Work Placement and the Program, evaluating the Program and for policy development and planning purposes in accordance with the Department’s processes and the Privacy and Data Protection Act 2014 (VIC).

  20. Upon request, the Host Employer must permit the Department and/or the Auditor General of Victoria access to accounting and employment records relating to the Work Placement.

  21. If the Host Employer breaches the Agreement or if the Department is of the reasonable opinion that:

    1. the Work Placement is not proceeding satisfactorily; or

    2. the Host Employer has engaged or may engage in any conduct which affects or may adversely affect the goodwill or reputation of the Host Employer, the Work Placement, the Department, a Minister, or the State,

    3. the Subsidy have not been expended solely on the Work Placement; or

    4. the Host Employer has provided any false or misleading information in the application process,

    the Department may in its absolute discretion:

    1. withhold any payment due to be made under the Subsidy

    2. require the repayment to the Delivery Partner of the whole or part of the Subsidy as may be determined by the Department in its absolute discretion; and/or

    3. by written notice terminate this Agreement.

  22. Where the Department is of the reasonable opinion that:

    1. there has been a significant deterioration in the financial circumstances of the Host Employer;

    2. the Host Employer is subject to an insolvency administration; or

    3. continued association with the Work Placement or the Host Employer may bring a Minister, the Department or the State into disrepute,

    the Department may in its absolute discretion, require the Delivery Partner to withhold, suspend, cancel or terminate any payment or payments due or to fall due and/or by written notice terminate the Agreement.

  23. The Department reserves the right to publicise the benefits accruing as the result of Work Placement. The Host Employer must cooperate with the Department in relation to all publicity associated with the Work Placement.

  24. The Department may include the name of the Host Employer in its annual report.

  25. The parties agree:

    1. there is no Taxable Supply under this Agreement;

    2. unless otherwise expressly stated, all amounts payable or consideration to be provided under or in accordance with this Agreement are exclusive of GST; and

    3. in the event GST is imposed on any supply made under or in accordance with this Agreement, the Host Employer will be responsible for payment of any GST liability imposed and will not seek reimbursement or compensation from either the Delivery Partner or the Department in relation to that liability or any costs associated with that liability.

  26. The Host Employer must maintain the following for the duration of this Agreement:

    1. WorkCover insurance (or equivalent) for an unlimited amount that shall include liability under statute or common law; and

    2. any other insurances as appropriate for the activities being carried out by the Host Employer, including (but not limited to) public liability and professional indemnity,

      3. and provide a copy of these insurance policies at the Department’s request.

  27. The Host Employer hereby indemnifies the Department, its officers, employees and agents against any cost, loss, liability or expense incurred by the Department arising from any act or omission of the Host Employer in connection with the Work Placement or breach of this Agreement by the Host Employer.

  28. Any variations to these conditions or to the Work Placement must be in writing and agreed by both parties, except where the Host Employer has made one or more further Applications and the Delivery Partner has sent one or more Additional Placement Confirmation Emails, in which event the Agreement will be varied to include the additional Work Placement/s specified in the Additional Placement Confirmation Emails.

  29. Each provision of these conditions (or part thereof) will, unless the context requires otherwise, be read and construed as a separate or severable provision so that if any provision or part thereof is void or otherwise unenforceable for any reason then that provision or part thereof, will be severed and the remainder will be read and construed as if the severable provision or part thereof, had never existed.

  30. The laws of the State of Victoria shall govern these conditions and the parties submit themselves to the jurisdiction of the courts in the State of Victoria.
