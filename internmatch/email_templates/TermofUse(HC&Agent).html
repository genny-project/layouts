1. Internship Placement Terms and Conditions


    1. Nature of Internship Placement

      The internship Placement is unpaid. The Internship Placement is a Vocational Placement and as such, the Itern has no entitlement to any remuneration in respect of the Internship Placement or work undertaken during the Internship Placement. The Host Company is under no obligation to provide the Itern with any remuneration during the term of, or in respect of any work performed by the Itern during, the Internship Placement.

      Interns are approved to only complete an Internship Placement with the above registered company or commonly controlled entities. Interns are not allowed to undertake as Internship Placement, employment or an unremunerated Internship Placement with a different Host Company.


    2. Internship Placement extension

      Under no circumstances is the Internship Placement to be extended beyond the specified Start and End Dates. Internship dates will be confirmed in the Internship Placement Aggrement which you can locate in InternMatch upon confirmation a specific Internship Placement.


    3. Commitment to training

      Host Companies and Interns must maintain the agreed Training Plan developed prior to the commencement of the Internship Placement. The Training Plan identifies the type of duties the Intern will be introduced to and as such, is a practical base that will ensure the Intern obtains relevant learning opportunities, while also ensuring that they devekop skills and knowledge that are relevant to your business.


    4. Supervision

      Host Companies must provide adequate supervision to the Intern Throughout the Internship Placement. This involves:

      • consistently monitoring your Intern's learning progress
      • appointing an adequately qualified staff member to the supervisor role
      • providing training as required
      • ensuring appropriate procedures and processes are in place that support a positive learning environment for the Intern
      • communicating any chandes or circumstances of the Internship Placement to Outcome.life immediately
      • ensuring that the Host Company has a demonstrated commitment to a workplace that is free from bullying and discrimation, and immediately reporting to Outcome.Life any complaint or incident involving the Intern in respect of bullying or discrimation in the workplace
      • providing an induction program, to ensure that the Intern is aware of and familiar with the obligations of safe work, policies and procedures that apply to the Host Company's business and operations, including the procedure for notifying health and safty issues at the Host Company; and
      • ensuring, as far as is reeasonably practicable, that the Intern is not exposed to risks to tehir health nad safety arising from the conduct of the undertaking of the Host Company


    5. Other obligations

      Outcome.Life appreciates the time involved in training and supporting the Interns. The Host Company must notify Outcome.Life immediately if any of the following circumstances change:

      • hours of work
      • attendance issues (i.e. sick days, punctuality issues, or other)
      • changes to the Training Plan (i.e. changes to the scope or objectives of the Internship Placement)
      • human resources issues that may affect the Internship Placement or supervision of the Intern. For example, the departure of the Intern's immediate supervisor.
      • any other changes.


  2. Obligations of Outcome.Life

    Outcome.Life acknowledges that it must:

    • provide an induction program, for the Intern who will undertake the Internship Placement, informing them of their general responsibility to behave in a safe manner in a workplace environment and comply with the requirements of occupational health and safety legislation; and
    • remain responsible for the overall control and disclipine of the Intern, apart from reasonable direction provided on a day to day basis by the Host Company to the Intern while they are undertaking the Internship Placement at the Host Company's premises.


  • Guidelines for Interns and Host Companies

    Adhering to the following guidelines will ensure a successful and a most rewarding experience for the Intern and the Host Company.


    1. Punctuality and attendence

      Outcome.Life will check with the Host Company and the Intern on the progress and attendence of the Intern on a regular basis. Any required leave ,ust be agreed to by Outcome.Life in advance. Any absence due to sickness must be reported to the Placement Consultant and Supervisor at the Host Company immediately.


    2. Dress code

      The Intern must conform to the dress code of the Host Company.


    3. Confidentiality

      Interns agree to keep all information and documents entrusted to them confidential and in accordance with Host Company procedures and ethics.


    4. Intellectual Property

      All Intellectual property in all materials created by the Intern or Host Company during the Internship Placement will be and remain the property of the Host Company.


    5. Misuse of Host Company facilities and propety

      Any misuse of Host Company facilities or property is prohibited and the Intern shall be liable for any expenses incured as a result of such misuse. It is the responsibility of the Intern to ensure that they understand Host Company policy regarding the use of mobile phones, Internet access and phone usage for personal use during Internship Placement hours. The Intern must conform to these policies.


    6. Conduct

      The Intern agrees to carry out all assigned tasks and to approach every opportunities provided by the Host Company with enthusiasm and professionalism to the best of their ability.


    7. Issues

      In the first instances, issues that arise should be discussed with the supervisor at the Host Company.


    8. Liability

      Outcome.Life provides an internship placement service and will use its best endeavours to place a suitable Intern with a Host Company, however, Outcome.Life is not responsible for the acts or omissions of an INtern while they are on a placement with the Host Company.

      The Host company acknowledge and agrees that the Intern is not an employee or agent of Outcome.Life.


    9. Early cessation of the Internship Placement

      The Host Company reserves the right to cease hosting the Intern prior to the End Date of the Internship Placement in circumstances where it reasonably believes there to be a legitimate reason for such cessation. The Host Company agrees to advise Outcome.Life, prior to any decision to cease hosting the Intern, of the reasons why it wishes to cease the Internship Placement and to only cease hosting th eIntern if the issue cannot be resolved. As this is a training opportunity, any issues whether poor performance or attitude are required to be identified and discussed with Outcome.Life so that the Intern is given an opportunity to improve.


  • Post Internship Employment

    • There is no cost to the Host Company for taking on an Interns.
    • To incentivise Outcome.Life to provide the highest quality interns that could result in the Host Coompany offering the Intern employment after the internship, the Host Company agrees to pay Outcome.Life a Post Internship Employment Fee.
    • The Host Company agrees to pay Outcome.Life $1,500 plus GST (being the "Post Internship Employment Fee"), if during the Internship or for a period of 6 months after the Internship has ended, the Host Company employs the Intern for at least 15 hours per week.
    • It should be noted that there is no expectation for the Host Company to offer employment to an Intern after thier internship has been completed.


  • Acknowledgement of Internship Relationship and Role of Both Parties.

    The parties acknowledge that:

    • Internship placements (workplace-based training or special program placements) are designed to assist Interns to provide a cultural/social experience and/or to develop skills relevant to their tertiary education, current occupation or field of study. It will involve the observation of tasks performed by employees of the Host Company and/or the performance of tasks under close supervision of the employees of the Host Company.
    • There is no intention on the part of the Host Company or the Intern to create an employment relationship as recognised under the law of Australia. It is accepted by the Intern that there is no employment relationship between the Host Company and the Intern.
    • The Host Company understands its obligations under Australian industrial relations laws if the Intern becomes an employee
    • The Host Company understands that workplace relations laws make it unlawful to employ an employee and not pay them, and that penalties may be imposed against any employer who breaches these laws.
    • The Host Company considers that this arrangement constitutes a vocational placement under the Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)
